## Opening: The real prospecting problem is conversion, not discovery

The supply of identifiable ultra-high-net-worth prospects in the Bay Area is not the constraint. San Francisco metro holds roughly 4,380 UHNW individuals (net worth above $30M) and 84 billionaires, the third-highest US billionaire count and the seventh-highest global density of ultra-wealthy individuals per capita (1). Roughly 66% of Silicon Valley tech workers are foreign-born, and about 45% of Bay Area tech startups have at least one foreign-born founder, which means cross-border family structure and multi-jurisdictional estate complexity are closer to the default than the exception (2).

Identification is solved. Conversion is not.

The typical senior associate or vice president at a UHNW advisory firm runs a templated outreach program. A list of 300 names is loaded into a CRM. Each name receives the same five-touch sequence: an introductory email, a LinkedIn note, a market-update forward, a soft event invitation, a breakfast ask. The template is fast, measurable, and feels like activity. It is also legible as a template to the prospect the moment they open it. Lengthening the cadence to ten or fifteen touches with AI-assisted personalization does not change the diagnosis; more template is still template. The result is a conversion rate that bottoms out somewhere below industry mean and a book of business that grows almost entirely by luck.

Prospects at this wealth tier are not short on advisor outreach. They are short on advisors who understand them before the first meeting.

*Relationship intelligence*, the operating frame of this case study, is the discipline that closes this gap. It is structured memory captured from attentive listening, cross-referenced with public signals, organized into a *Prospect File* per relationship that drives one decision: the right opening to the right person at the right moment.

This piece lays out the operating framework, the underlying schema, four mock Bay Area tech profiles staggered across the prospecting pipeline (targeting, outreach, discovery, advancement), and the discipline that distinguishes relationship intelligence from data harvesting. Each profile shows the Prospect File captured at a decision point; together they trace the pipeline.

## Strategic Memo: Relationship intelligence beats ocean-boiling

### Why templated outreach persists despite poor conversion

Template outreach persists because it solves a problem the advisor has, not a problem the prospect has. The advisor needs throughput, pipeline optics, and a reason to report activity to the team lead. A sequenced 300-name campaign produces all three. The prospect gets an email that reads like the last six emails they received.

Research on elite advisory practice is consistent on this point. Investor-side studies of advisor selection show that wealthy investors weight five criteria when evaluating advisors (People, Philosophy, Process, Performance, Fees), and that the rank-ordering varies meaningfully across prospects (3). An outreach program that opens on Performance when the prospect secretly weights People is dead on arrival. Template outreach cannot adjust on that axis because it does not know which axis matters.

The same body of research argues that *relationship alpha*, defined as durable fit between advisor and client, is more predictive of long-term retention than investment alpha (4). The practical implication is uncomfortable: prospecting at UHNW scale is a fit-diagnosis exercise first and a capability demonstration second.

### What the Prospect File actually solves

The Prospect File reframes top-of-funnel work as fit-diagnosis first, capability demonstration second. Templated outreach fails because it cannot adjust to the prospect's heaviest weighted P; the Prospect File's discipline is to identify that P before the first touch and shape every subsequent move around it.

The case for this reframing rests on a load-bearing data point: 67% of affluent prospects who hired an advisor already knew someone with that advisor before the relationship began (5). Cold outreach is not the dominant pathway to UHNW conversion. Relational warmth is. The Prospect File treats the relational graph (centers of influence, alumni networks, fellow board members, trusted COIs) as a first-class citizen, not as an afterthought to product capability.

The three layers (*Profile* for what the prospect is, *Decision Log* for what the banker has thought and done, *Relational Graph* for the connective tissue of COIs and pattern matches) together convert a prospect's signal set into a single-sentence outreach thesis: the right opening to the right person at the right moment.

Prospect Files together form a *pipeline*: qualification at the top, multi-meeting depth in the middle, existing-client relationships at the far end. The same Prospect File travels with each relationship across stages; the discipline that won the relationship is the discipline that keeps it.

### From lead generation to relationship intelligence

Relationship intelligence reframes the top of the funnel. The question is not "how many names can I touch this quarter" but "what do I know about this one prospect that makes my first outreach impossible to mistake for a template?"

Inputs are two kinds of signal. The first is conversational: what the prospect volunteered at a dinner, a panel, a child's school event; what they avoided; what they returned to twice; who they referenced by first name versus last name. The second is public: filings, board seats, recent liquidity events, philanthropic activity, civic involvement, published interviews, and the connective tissue between these.

Neither signal type is useful alone. A standalone public-data pull produces a dossier. A standalone conversational note produces a memory. The cross-reference produces an outreach decision.

### The workflow in practice

Every first encounter logs to the Prospect File within 24 hours. Conversational signals go in verbatim. Public signals refresh monthly via a research-compression pass. Every prospect carries a single-sentence outreach thesis answering one question: what is the opening that is impossible to mistake for a template? Outreach moves only when the thesis is clean.

## The Prospect File Architecture

The Prospect File is a three-layer structured memory: *Profile*, *Decision Log*, *Relational Graph*. Each prospect has one file; the file persists across relationship stages; the collection of files forms the pipeline. The architecture is the same at the qualification stage as it is five meetings later; what changes is how densely each layer is populated.

### Layer 1 — Profile

What the prospect is. Fourteen categories, approximately 150 fields. Categories span core identity and residences; family and multi-generational structure; education across generations; wealth story; professional history and boards; cultural origin and orientation; food, beverage, dietary; sports, fitness, hobbies, travel; personality and communication; philanthropy; network, memberships, and centers of influence; conversational signals and behavioral notes; sensitive information volunteered only; trigger events and timing markers.

Each field carries one of two tags. *Decision-driving* [DD] fields have explicit if/then logic: this field's value drives this specific outreach move. *Reference* [REF] fields support decision-driving fields without independent decision logic. The schema's design rule: if a field cannot drive an outreach decision, it does not belong.

Representative decision-driving fields and their if/then logic:

- **Liquidity event history** (Cat 4). *If 6 to 18 months pre-event, lead with pre-liquidity planning (10b5-1 sequencing, QSBS stacking, exchange funds); if past 18 months post-event, that framing is late.*
- **Spouse financial agency** (Cat 2). *If spouse is the primary decision-maker, prospect-only first meeting is a wasted meeting; pivot to couple-format venue.*
- **Family decision-making cultural mode** (Cat 6). *If Collective Harmony, no commitment possible until the group aligns; if Honor, route early outreach through the patriarch even if the prospect is the operator generation; if Individualist, the prospect is authorized to decide.*
- **First-vs-third-person wealth language** (Cat 12). *If the prospect consistently speaks of inherited wealth in the third person ("the money," "the trust"), product pitch is premature; first mandate is psychological.*
- **Known forward triggers** (Cat 14). *If a planned IPO, M&A close, or foundation launch is approaching, time the outreach to the trigger. A trust conversation 60 days before a planned IPO is timely; six months after is late.*

### Layer 2 — Decision Log

Per prospect, dated chronological journal of what the banker has thought and done. Six entry types: *Inference drawn* (what an observed signal implies about the prospect); *Move considered* (menu of outreach options evaluated, including options rejected, logged with rationale); *Move taken* (what was executed); *Outcome* (signal the move produced); *Question to surface* (open questions not yet answered); *Pattern match flag* (recognition that this prospect's signal set resembles an Existing Client). Each entry dates, names trigger, cross-references Layer 1 fields updated and Layer 3 elements involved.

### Layer 3 — Relational Graph

The connective tissue. Two sub-tables. The *COI Ledger* holds one record per Center of Influence the banker directly knows (outside counsel, estate counsel, school development officer, fellow board director, trusted mutual contact). Each record captures identity, relationship origin, temperature, last touch and next scheduled touch, cadence, past introductions made and their outcomes, what the COI needs from the banker in return, a reciprocity ledger, and style notes. COIs cross multiple prospects and multiple relationship stages within the banker's own book; a single COI record supports many files.

The *Pattern Map*, per prospect, flags one to three Existing Clients whose signal set this prospect resembles, plus an anti-pattern Existing Client whose situation looked similar but played out differently. Pattern matches are drawn only from the banker's own directly-served clients; client information protected by firm confidentiality policy and regulatory privacy frameworks stays within the trust boundary that earned it. The Pattern Map reflects the banker's own book, not a firm-wide client database. Each pattern match captures what worked there, what failed, what to carry forward, and what to avoid. Pattern-matching is the first move of any new outreach decision, not the last.

### The Inference Layer

The mental model that turns Layer 1, Layer 2, and Layer 3 data into a specific outreach move. Without it, the Prospect File is a data dictionary; with it, the Prospect File is a decision engine.

Five rules for reading the file:

1. **Weight is uneven.** One or two signals carry disproportionate weight; the rest is context. The banker's first job is to identify the load-bearing signal, not to triangulate equally across 150 fields.
2. **Absence is signal.** Avoidance, silence, omission are data. The prospect who never mentions a former co-founder is communicating something about that relationship.
3. **Contradictions are diagnostic.** When Layer 1 fields contradict, the contradiction is the planning conversation.
4. **The strongest opener routes through the heaviest signal.** Outreach built around the second-heaviest signal is competent but generic. The template's failure mode.
5. **Pattern-match before drafting.** Every first-touch draft starts from Layer 3, not from a blank page. If no pattern match exists, this file is a new archetype; flag it and draft from first principles.

Three discovery answers are the highest-yield early-meeting questions:

- "What is most important about this wealth to you?" (values-first opener)
- "If we were meeting three years from now, what would have to happen for this to feel successful?" (the three-year question)
- "What has been your experience with prior advisors? How did you choose them? What did you learn?" (selection diagnostic)

Once all three answers are captured in Layer 1 Cat 12, the outreach thesis is close to determined. Until all three are captured, outreach is tentative; once captured, outreach is decisive.

### Provenance discipline

Every datum in the Prospect File carries a source. Two top-level buckets: *Public* information (online records, published content, pre-meeting referral context from a COI) and *Direct* interaction (seated conversation, phone call, dinner, organized per touchpoint). The Prospect File holds nothing that was not either volunteered by the prospect or drawn from the public record. Sensitive information (Cat 13) stays empty by default and is entered only when the prospect themselves volunteers. The discipline is the defense: when AI synthesis of structured public records gets cheaper, the source-of-each-datum check is what holds the line between signal gathering and intrusion.

## Four Prospect Files

Four mock profiles staggered across the prospecting pipeline, each captured at the moment of an outreach decision.

### Prospect 1: Exited tech founder (post-M2)

**Relationship stage.** Post-second-meeting. Two seated conversations complete: M1 on 2025-03-12 (intro via COI 4, former outside counsel on the acquisition) and M2 on 2025-04-02 as a Stanford Dish run with specialist estate counsel COI 7. Third meeting scheduled 2025-05-05 as breakfast with his spouse present, at the prospect's own invitation. Spouse inclusion at prospect-initiated scheduling is the key progression marker.

**Archetype.** Exited tech founder, mid-career, post-acquisition. Indian-American naturalized US citizen.

**Profile.** 43 years old. Born Maharashtra; came to the US in 2000 for college (Berkeley CS 2004). Hindu, moderately observant. Single founder of a vertical SaaS company (2014–2025); sold to a publicly traded acquirer January 2025 for $180M cash-and-stock, with 40% rolled into acquirer stock under a 24-month lockup releasing in tranches (September 2026 and March 2027). Net worth tier: above $100M. Existing structures: revocable living trust 2018; no dynasty, GRAT, CRT, DAF, or foundation. QSBS claimed for own shares but not stacked across spouse or trusts (gap). Single wirehouse advisor since 2020 plus firm-based CPA; no SBL line. Menlo Park residence, personal name joint tenancy. Married 2012; spouse is a 2nd-gen Indian-American MBA and former product lead, full-time parent to two sons (12 and 9) at a Mandarin-immersion school in Palo Alto. His parents (retired physicians) live in Cupertino since 2019, after his father's cardiac event. One sister in London. Morning runner, vegetarian, tea drinker. Five Ps inferred: People > Philosophy > Process > Fees > Performance.

**Decision log.** At M1 he mentioned his mother twice unprompted, his co-founder zero times, and named his Pune boarding school before Berkeley. Inference: family weight exceeds founder weight; co-founder omission reads as a wound, not oversight; identity is heritage-first. Three moves considered: a memo on lockup tax sequencing (rejected for leading with founder identity); a dinner via a mutual school parent (rejected because he declines unknown-group dinners); a warm-intro request to specialist estate counsel COI 7 for a 7am Stanford Dish run (selected, because it routes through the heaviest signal and opens on generational trust for his mother's long-term care, not on a pitch). M2 executed the run. Within ten minutes he volunteered his father's retirement; his founder biography never arose. He volunteered his mother's 2015 breast cancer remission and his spouse's parents' planning gap. Prior-advisor diagnostic: "fine but never proactive." M3 requested with his spouse present.

**Pattern match.** Existing Client 1 (post-acquisition founder, family-first identity, observant household). What worked there: morning-run venue picked by the intro source; opening question about the father's estate; six-month gap before any product mention; spouse included at M2 at her invitation. Anti-pattern: Existing Client 5 (surface match but G2 inheritor of an operating company, family-first frame already saturated by an existing multi-generational family office). Lesson: family-first identity is necessary but not sufficient; verify Built versus Inherited wealth before applying the play.

**Outreach play.** Warm intro via COI 4, by email with a one-sentence banker-drafted context paragraph. Opening angle: structural memo on how the September 2026 lockup release interacts with a generational trust accommodating his mother's long-term care. Timing: early Q3 2025, approximately 12 months pre-lockup, squarely in the Kochis 6–18-month pre-event planning window. Avoids Diwali (November) and his India trip (February 2026). Second-meeting artifact: two-page structure memo with the QSBS stacking gap surfaced diagnostically (not pitched) and trust architecture covering parents, spouse, and sons.

**Non-obvious signal that drove the decision.** His heritage biography and his mother's care lead every meeting. Routing first material outreach through his parents' estate, not the exit proceeds, is the only opener that survives Rule 4.

**Provenance.** Public sources covered identity, residence (Santa Clara County property records), spouse and children, education, founding and 2025 acquisition (acquirer press release plus SEC Form 4), board seats, and COI 4's pre-meeting referral briefing (his father's cardiac event, parents' move, sister in London, prior wirehouse dissatisfaction without specifics, preference for morning-run venues). Direct interaction at M1 captured observance level, food and wine preferences, existing financial structures, DAF details, a stated boundary ("I want one person I trust"), and his values discovery answer. Direct interaction at M2 captured his father's retirement, his mother's cancer remission, his spouse's parents' planning gap, his planning fear, his regret, his risk posture, and the prior-advisor diagnostic.

## Extensibility: The schema beyond Bay Area tech

The four prospects above live in Bay Area tech. The schema itself generalizes. Categories stay stable across verticals; specific fields swap to match the archetype.

For a **Latin American multi-generational family office**, the *Liquidity event history* field becomes *Family-business operating entity and exit posture* (most wealth stays in operating assets, not in discrete liquidity events), and *Ties to home country* expands to *Jurisdictional footprint across generations*, tracking the pattern common to families with branches across Mexico City, Miami, Madrid, and Bogotá.

For a **legacy industrial family**, *Professional history and board work* becomes *Family council role and operating-company seat*, and the *Philanthropic interests* field is reframed around the century-old foundation the family likely already operates.

For a **specialty medical practice family**, *Asset concentration* shifts from founder stock to practice equity and real estate; *Conversational signals* pick up practice-succession language (partnership buy-in timing, whether a child will take over the practice) that drives outreach toward continuity, not diversification.

The rule is the same across verticals: if a field cannot drive an outreach decision, it does not belong in the schema.

The framework above synthesizes the practitioner literature named below into one working approach.
