## Opening: The real prospecting problem is conversion, not discovery

The supply of identifiable ultra-high-net-worth prospects in the Bay Area is not the constraint. San Francisco metro holds roughly 4,380 UHNW individuals (net worth above $30M) and 84 billionaires, the third-highest US billionaire count and the seventh-highest global density of ultra-wealthy individuals per capita (1). Roughly 66% of Silicon Valley tech workers are foreign-born, and about 45% of Bay Area tech startups have at least one foreign-born founder, which means cross-border family structure and multi-jurisdictional estate complexity are closer to the default than the exception (2).

Identification is solved. Conversion is not.

Templated outreach is standard practice at UHNW advisory firms. A typical sequence runs through email, LinkedIn, market-update shares, event invitations, and meeting requests. The template is fast, measurable, and feels like activity. It is also legible as a template to the prospect the moment they open it. Lengthening the sequence with AI-assisted personalization does not change the diagnosis; more template is still template. Conversion rates suffer accordingly, and the resulting book grows mostly by chance.

Prospects at this wealth tier are not short on advisor outreach. They are short on advisors who understand them before the first meeting.

*Relationship intelligence*, the operating frame of this case study, is the discipline that closes this gap. It is structured memory captured from attentive listening, cross-referenced with public signals, organized into a *Prospect File* per relationship that drives one decision: the right opening to the right person at the right moment.

This piece lays out the operating framework, the underlying schema, four mock Bay Area tech profiles staggered across the prospecting pipeline (targeting, outreach, discovery, advancement), and the discipline that distinguishes relationship intelligence from data harvesting. Each profile shows the Prospect File captured at a decision point; together they trace the pipeline.

## Methodology

### What the Prospect File actually solves

The Prospect File reframes top-of-funnel work as fit-diagnosis first, capability demonstration second.

Wealthy investors weigh five criteria when evaluating advisors — People, Philosophy, Process, Performance, Fees — and the rank-ordering varies meaningfully across prospects (3). Outreach calibrated to the heaviest weighted P converts; outreach calibrated to the wrong P does not. The Prospect File's discipline is to identify that P before the first touch and shape every subsequent move around it.

Durable fit between advisor and client (*relationship alpha*) is more predictive of long-term retention than investment performance (4). Prospecting at UHNW scale is a fit-diagnosis exercise first and a capability demonstration second.

67% of affluent prospects who hired an advisor already knew someone with that advisor before the relationship began (5). The Prospect File treats the relational graph (centers of influence, alumni networks, fellow board members, trusted COIs) as a first-class citizen.

The three layers (*Profile* for what the prospect is, *Decision Log* for what the banker has thought and done, *Relational Graph* for the connective tissue of COIs and pattern matches) together convert a prospect's signal set into a single-sentence outreach thesis: the right opening to the right person at the right moment.

Prospect Files together form a *pipeline*: qualification at the top, multi-meeting depth in the middle, existing-client relationships at the far end. The same Prospect File travels with each relationship across stages; the discipline that won the relationship is the discipline that keeps it.

### What the Prospect File takes in

Inputs are two kinds of signal. The first is conversational: what the prospect volunteered at a dinner, a panel, a child's school event; what they avoided; what they returned to twice; who they referenced by first name versus last name. The second is public: filings, board seats, recent liquidity events, philanthropic activity, civic involvement, published interviews, and the connective tissue between these.

Neither signal type is useful alone. A standalone public-data pull produces a dossier. A standalone conversational note produces a memory. The cross-reference produces an outreach decision.

### The workflow in practice

Every first encounter logs to the Prospect File within 24 hours. Conversational signals go in verbatim. Public signals refresh monthly via a research-compression pass. Every prospect carries a single-sentence outreach thesis answering one question: what is the right opening for this specific prospect? Outreach moves only when the thesis is clean.

## The Prospect File Architecture

The Prospect File is a three-layer structured memory: *Profile*, *Decision Log*, *Relational Graph*. One file per prospect, persisting across stages; the collection of files forms the pipeline.

### Layer 1 — Profile

Fourteen categories, approximately 150 fields:

1. Core identity and residences
2. Family, multi-generational
3. Education, across generations
4. Wealth story
5. Professional history and boards
6. Cultural origin and orientation
7. Food, beverage, dietary
8. Sports, fitness, hobbies, travel
9. Personality and communication
10. Philanthropy
11. Network, memberships, and centers of influence
12. Conversational signals and behavioral notes
13. Sensitive information (volunteered only)
14. Trigger events and timing markers

Each field carries one of two tags. *Decision-driving* [DD] fields have explicit if/then logic that drives a specific outreach move — for example, a prospect 6 to 18 months pre-liquidity gets pre-event tax-alpha planning; a prospect with primary spousal financial agency gets a couple-format meeting. *Reference* [REF] fields support DD fields without independent decision logic. The schema's design rule: if a field cannot drive an outreach decision, it does not belong.

### Layer 2 — Decision Log

Per prospect, dated journal of what the banker has thought and done. Six entry types: *inferences drawn* from observed signals; *moves considered* (including options rejected, with rationale); *moves taken*; *outcomes*; *questions to surface* in the next meeting; *pattern match flags* against Existing Clients.

### Layer 3 — Relational Graph

Two sub-tables. The *COI Ledger* holds one record per Center of Influence the banker directly knows — origin, temperature, cadence, past introductions and their outcomes, reciprocity ledger, and what the COI needs in return. COIs cross multiple prospects within the banker's own book.

The *Pattern Map*, per prospect, flags one to three Existing Clients whose signal set this prospect resembles, plus an anti-pattern Existing Client whose situation looked similar but played out differently. Pattern matches are drawn only from the banker's own directly-served clients; client information protected by firm confidentiality and regulatory privacy frameworks stays within the trust boundary that earned it. The Pattern Map reflects the banker's own book, not a firm-wide database.

### The Inference Layer

The mental model that turns Layer 1, Layer 2, and Layer 3 data into a specific outreach move. Five rules:

1. **Weight is uneven.** One or two signals carry disproportionate weight; the rest is context.
2. **Absence is signal.** Avoidance, silence, omission are data.
3. **Contradictions are diagnostic.** When fields contradict, the contradiction is the planning conversation.
4. **The strongest opener routes through the heaviest signal.** Outreach built around the second-heaviest signal is competent but generic.
5. **Pattern-match before drafting.** Every first-touch draft starts from Layer 3.

Three discovery answers are the highest-yield early-meeting questions: "What is most important about this wealth to you?", "If we were meeting three years from now, what would have to happen for this to feel successful?", and "What has been your experience with prior advisors? How did you choose them? What did you learn?" Once captured in Layer 1 Cat 12, the outreach thesis is close to determined.

### Provenance discipline

Every datum in the Prospect File carries a source. *Public* information includes online records, published content, and pre-meeting referral context from a COI. *Direct* interaction includes any seated conversation, phone call, or dinner. Sensitive information (Cat 13) stays empty by default and is entered only when the prospect themselves volunteers. The discipline is the defense: when AI synthesis of structured public records gets cheaper, the source-of-each-datum check is what holds the line between signal gathering and intrusion.

## Four Prospect Files

Four mock profiles staggered across the prospecting pipeline, each captured at the moment of an outreach decision.

### Prospect 1: Exited tech founder (post-M2)

**Relationship stage.** Post-second-meeting. Two seated conversations complete: M1 on 2025-03-12 (intro via COI 4, former outside counsel on the acquisition) and M2 on 2025-04-02 as a Stanford Dish run with specialist estate counsel COI 7. Third meeting scheduled 2025-05-05 as breakfast with his spouse present, at the prospect's own invitation. Spouse inclusion at prospect-initiated scheduling is the key progression marker.

**Archetype.** Exited tech founder, mid-career, post-acquisition. Indian-American naturalized US citizen.

**Profile.** 43 years old. Born Maharashtra; came to the US in 2000 for college (Berkeley CS 2004). Hindu, moderately observant. Single founder of a vertical SaaS company (2014–2025); sold to a publicly traded acquirer January 2025 for $180M cash-and-stock, with 40% rolled into acquirer stock under a 24-month lockup releasing in tranches (September 2026 and March 2027). Net worth tier: above $100M. Existing structures: revocable living trust 2018; no dynasty, GRAT, CRT, DAF, or foundation. QSBS claimed for own shares but not stacked across spouse or trusts (gap). Single wirehouse advisor since 2020 plus firm-based CPA; no SBL line. Menlo Park residence, personal name joint tenancy. Married 2012; spouse is a 2nd-gen Indian-American MBA and former product lead, full-time parent to two sons (12 and 9) at a Mandarin-immersion school in Palo Alto. His parents (retired physicians) live in Cupertino since 2019, after his father's cardiac event. One sister in London. Morning runner, vegetarian, tea drinker. Five Ps inferred: People > Philosophy > Process > Fees > Performance.

**Decision log.** At M1 he mentioned his mother twice unprompted, his co-founder zero times, and named his Pune boarding school before Berkeley. Inference: family weight exceeds founder weight; co-founder omission reads as a wound, not oversight; identity is heritage-first. Three moves considered: a memo on lockup tax sequencing (rejected for leading with founder identity); a dinner via a mutual school parent (rejected because he declines unknown-group dinners); a warm-intro request to specialist estate counsel COI 7 for a 7am Stanford Dish run (selected, because it routes through the heaviest signal and opens on generational trust for his mother's long-term care, not on a pitch). M2 executed the run. Within ten minutes he volunteered his father's retirement; his founder biography never arose. He volunteered his mother's 2015 breast cancer remission and his spouse's parents' planning gap. His planning fear surfaced in his own words ("I don't want my parents to need something I haven't thought of"), along with his regret ("wishes had set up a trust for his parents pre-exit") and his risk posture ("conservative, because I got lucky once and I don't want to be lucky twice"). Prior-advisor diagnostic: "fine but never proactive." M3 requested with his spouse present.

**Pattern match.** Existing Client 1 (post-acquisition founder, family-first identity, observant household). What worked there: morning-run venue picked by the intro source; opening question about the father's estate; six-month gap before any product mention; spouse included at M2 at her invitation. Anti-pattern: Existing Client 5 (surface match but G2 inheritor of an operating company, family-first frame already saturated by an existing multi-generational family office). Lesson: family-first identity is necessary but not sufficient; verify Built versus Inherited wealth before applying the play.

**Outreach play.** Warm intro via COI 4, by email with a one-sentence banker-drafted context paragraph. Opening angle: structural memo on how the September 2026 lockup release interacts with a generational trust accommodating his mother's long-term care. Timing: early Q3 2025, approximately 12 months pre-lockup, squarely in the Kochis 6–18-month pre-event planning window. Avoids Diwali (November) and his India trip (February 2026). Second-meeting artifact: two-page structure memo with the QSBS stacking gap surfaced diagnostically (not pitched) and trust architecture covering parents, spouse, and sons.

**Non-obvious signal that drove the decision.** His heritage biography and his mother's care lead every meeting. Routing first material outreach through his parents' estate, not the exit proceeds, is the only opener that survives Rule 4.

**Provenance.** Public sources covered identity, residence (Santa Clara County property records), spouse and children, education, founding and 2025 acquisition (acquirer press release plus SEC Form 4), board seats, and COI 4's pre-meeting referral briefing (his father's cardiac event, parents' move, sister in London, prior wirehouse dissatisfaction without specifics, preference for morning-run venues). Direct interaction at M1 captured observance level, food and wine preferences, existing financial structures, DAF details, a stated boundary ("I want one person I trust"), and his values discovery answer. Direct interaction at M2 captured his father's retirement, his mother's cancer remission, his spouse's parents' planning gap, his planning fear, his regret, his risk posture, and the prior-advisor diagnostic.

**Archetype.** Foreign-born first-gen operator, mid-career, post-secondary-sale, with a multi-jurisdictional family-business lineage in parallel to her built US exit. French.

**Profile.** 51 years old, born Paris to multi-generational French parents. French citizen by birth, naturalized US 2018; both passports active. What worked there: opening at her selected cafe; scoping the first written proposal as a multi-country coordination memo rather than a US-trust pitch; inviting the spouse at M3 not M1; pacing the relationship across a six-month arc with two written artifacts and one joint meeting with the Paris-side notary. The "heaviest signal routes through current operators" play that fits Prospect 2 would not fit Existing Client 7 because the operators had changed. **Outreach play.** Warm intro-continuation via COI 9, by email in French, with a one-sentence banker-drafted context paragraph and a French subject line. No firm named. One paragraph flags the tax-friction regret and proposes a coordinated unwind across 18 months.

**Non-obvious signal that drove the decision.** Her active board seat at the family jewelry business since 2017, combined with the 2028 succession horizon (her mother's 80th birthday) she names as the family-internal deadline, is the load-bearing signal. Any opener anchored on the US exit misreads her as fintech founder first and family principal second. Inverting the anchor (November 2028 family birthday, not September 2026 US lock release) is the only opener that survives Rule 4. Her prior-advisor postmortem ("they stopped knowing what was going on at home") frames "home" as the family-business operating side, not the Atherton household; the coordinated cross-border continuity frame is the direct structural answer.

**Provenance.** Public sources covered identity, passports, Atherton (San Mateo County records), Paris SCI (French registry), spouse background, children's schools, education (HEC Paris, Harvard Kennedy), fintech co-founding and 2023 secondary sale (announcement press + cap-table filings), board seats, op-ed publishing, and COI 9's pre-meeting referral context (household's bilingual register, chief-of-staff calendar control, prior walk-out from a firm-branded conference room, Philosophy-dominant disposition on governance). Direct interaction at M1 captured travel rhythm, cuisine and venue preferences, existing financial structures including the *mandat à effet posthume*, the multi-advisor no-quarterback arrangement, her risk posture ("we preserve, we don't gamble"), and all three discovery answers. Direct interaction at M2 captured the revision request on the pre-read, the older sister's exclusion from the succession thinking, the tax-friction regret, the mother's 2024 fall, the succession-fracture fear, and the M3 request with her spouse present.

**Pattern match.** Existing Client 9 (pre-IPO early employee at a different large Bay Area tech company, Mexican-American, first-in-family-wealth, supporting parents through pre-IPO earnings, Catholic-lapsed, reserved-analytical-skeptical). **Non-obvious signal that drove the decision.** The load-bearing signal is not the wealth event (the obvious public trigger); it is the first-in-family-wealth posture, which dictates that the entire outreach must avoid wealth-management vocabulary at M1. The second load-bearing signal is the parents-support pattern, which marks the heaviest discovery surface but cannot be raised by the banker; it must surface from him. The third is the cultural community signal: the COI 15 channel is the only one that survives Rule 4, because a Stanford-CS-classmate channel reads as professional but not community-trusted, and a cold-email channel collapses the frame.

## Extensibility: The schema beyond Bay Area tech

The four prospects above live in Bay Area tech. The schema itself generalizes. Categories stay stable across verticals; specific fields swap to match the archetype.

For a **Latin American multi-generational family office**, the *Liquidity event history* field becomes *Family-business operating entity and exit posture* (most wealth stays in operating assets, not in discrete liquidity events), and *Ties to home country* expands to *Jurisdictional footprint across generations*, tracking the pattern common to families with branches across Mexico City, Miami, Madrid, and Bogotá.

For a **legacy industrial family**, *Professional history and board work* becomes *Family council role and operating-company seat*, and the *Philanthropic interests* field is reframed around the century-old foundation the family likely already operates.

For a **specialty medical practice family**, *Asset concentration* shifts from founder stock to practice equity and real estate; *Conversational signals* pick up practice-succession language (partnership buy-in timing, whether a child will take over the practice) that drives outreach toward continuity, not diversification.

The rule is the same across verticals: if a field cannot drive an outreach decision, it does not belong in the schema.

The framework above synthesizes the practitioner literature named below into one working approach.
