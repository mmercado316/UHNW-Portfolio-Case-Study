## Practitioner mental-model layer

1. Charlotte Beyer, *Wealth Management Unwrapped* (Wiley, rev. 2017). https://www.amazon.com/Wealth-Management-Unwrapped-Revised-Expanded/dp/1119403693. The Five Ps framework (People, Philosophy, Process, Performance, Fees) and the practitioner instruction to have prospects rank-order them as a diagnostic.
2. Charlotte Beyer, "Relationship Alpha: The Emerging Competitive Advantage in Wealth Management," CFA Institute Research Foundation Brief, 2019. https://rpc.cfainstitute.org/research/foundation/2019/beyer-brief. The argument that durable advisor-client fit is more predictive of long-term retention than investment alpha.
3. Michael Kitces interview with Charlotte Beyer, Financial Advisor Success podcast. https://www.kitces.com/blog/charlotte-beyer-institute-private-investors-podcast-ipi-wealth-management-unwrapped/. The discovery question on prior advisor experience as the highest-yielding first-meeting diagnostic.
4. James Grubman, *Strangers in Paradise: How Families Adapt to Wealth Across Generations* (2013). https://jamesgrubman.com/books/. The "immigrants to wealth" model for first-generation wealth holders (denial / assimilation / integration).
5. Jaffe & Grubman, *Cross Cultures: How Global Families Negotiate Change Across Generations* (2016). https://dennisjaffe.com/cross-cultures-how-global-families-negotiate-change-across-generations/. Three-culture model for cross-border families (Individualist, Collective Harmony, Honor).
6. Dennis Jaffe, *Borrowed from Your Grandchildren: The Evolution of 100-Year Family Enterprises* (Wiley, 2020). https://dennisjaffe.com/announcing-publication-of-borrowed-from-your-grandchildren-evolution-of-100-year-family-enterprises/. Generative families; non-financial wealth by G2.
7. Tim Kochis, *Managing Concentrated Stock Wealth: An Advisor's Guide to Building Customized Solutions* (Bloomberg, 2015). https://www.amazon.com/Managing-Concentrated-Stock-Wealth-Customized/dp/1119131588. The canonical argument that most tax-alpha opportunities exist pre-liquidity-event.
8. Tim Kochis, *Wealth Management: A Concise Guide to Financial Planning and Investment Management for Wealthy Clients* (CCH, 2nd ed.). Goals-first, products-last discipline.
9. Thayer Willis, *Navigating the Dark Side of Wealth*. https://www.thayerwillis.com/. Wealth-psychology framework identifying inheritor identity patterns; diagnostic significance of third-person wealth language.
10. Kitces Financial Advisor Success Episode #474, Blair duQuesnay on UHNW service. https://www.kitces.com/blog/blair-duquesnay-ritholtz-wealth-management-ultra-high-net-worth-firm-service-skills-experience-team-planning/. UHNW fear is catastrophic mistake, not running out of money.

## Operational craft layer

11. Russ Alan Prince & Brett Van Bortel, *The Ultimate Rainmaker: The Ultra-Wealthy Adviser* (2024 ed.). COI methodology; referral-engine construction.
12. Russ Alan Prince et al., *Optimizing the Financial Lives of Clients* (2023). How CPAs and other professional advisors decide to refer.
13. Russ Alan Prince, "The Whole Client Model," Financial Advisor Magazine. Seven-category profiling for high-net-worth clients.
14. Dan Allison on Kitces Financial Advisor Success podcast #447. https://www.kitces.com/blog/coi-centers-of-influence-finanancial-advisor-relationship-referrals-strategy-prospects/. Highest-signal operational source on COI work: referral-risk opener; client-feedback loop; quarterly mutual-education meeting; three-meeting framework for COI relationships; capacity-signaling.
15. John Bowen (CEG Worldwide), "The Wealth Management Meeting Process: Screen, Discover, Present." https://www.cegworldwide.com/blog/preeminent-financial-advisor-052-bxpfe. Five values-first questions; advisor as general manager, not quarterback; 84% want emotional connection first.
16. Kitces with John Bowen, "What HNW Prospects Really Want." https://www.kitces.com/blog/high-net-worth-hnw-prospects-advisor-services-ceg-insights-data-john-bowen/. CEG Insights data on what HNW prospects actually evaluate.
17. Matt Oechsli, "Anatomy of an Effective LinkedIn Prospecting Message"; "Generate Referrals with This Simple Phrase"; "The #1 Reason Affluent Prospects Select Their Advisor" (67% of affluent hires already know someone with the advisor). Oechsli Institute research collection. https://oechsli.com/. Load-bearing data point for the Prospect File's treatment of the relational graph as a first-class citizen.
18. Matt Oechsli, "Financial Advisor Referral Tips: The Factor of Six Rule." https://oechsli.com/financial-advisor-referral-tips-the-factor-of-six-rule/. The Factor of Six is a referral-capacity rule (every top client has the network to introduce an advisor to ~6 similar-profile prospects over the relationship's life), NOT a touchpoint cadence rule.
19. Oechsli Institute, "2023 Affluent Research: It's All About Relationships." https://oechsli.com/2023-affluent-research-its-all-about-relationships/. Survey of 1,005 affluent clients ($500K+ investable): 80% consider their advisor a friend; 64% of friends made 3+ referrals in the prior year vs. 35% of non-friends; 75% uncomfortable with a generic referral *ask* but ~80% will facilitate a specific personal introduction.
20. Michael Kitces, "The 5-Step Not-Cheesy Prospecting Process." https://www.kitces.com/blog/prospect-process-advisor-value-client-relationship-trust-building/. Initial Inquiry → Learn More Call → Discovery Meeting → 1-Page Plan → Plan Presentation as a canonical advisor sales pipeline.
21. Michael Kitces, "Advisor Sales And Marketing KPIs: Tracking Activity And Prospect Sales Pipeline." https://www.kitces.com/blog/financial-advisor-marketing-kpis-tracking-activity-prospect-sales-pipeline/. Named pipeline stages for advisor BD.
22. Michael Kitces, "Best Advisor Marketing Strategies: Client Acquisition Cost Efficient Marketing." https://www.kitces.com/blog/advisor-marketing-strategy-client-acquisition-cost-efficient-marketing-time-dollar-cost/. 2024 conversion data across marketing channels (digital ads, podcasts, webinars, email, social).
23. WealthManagement.com, "Why Every Advisor Should Know the Factor of Six Rule," July 2019. https://www.wealthmanagement.com/prospecting/why-every-advisor-should-know-factor-six-rule. The Factor of Six referral-capacity rule introduced for a broader audience.
24. WealthManagement.com, "Courting Centers of Influence." Six named-advisor case studies on COI relationship-building.
25. Carl Richards / Kitces, "When the Big Prospect Doesn't Want Your Financial Plan." Method-vs-goal separation; Sullivan's three-year question.
26. Rob Knapp, *The Supernova Advisor: Crossing the Invisible Bridge to Exceptional Client Service and Consistent Growth* (Wiley, 2008). The 12-4-2 service model.
27. David J. Mullen Jr., *The Million-Dollar Financial Advisor: Powerful Lessons and Proven Strategies from Top Producers* (AMACOM, 2009). Niche marketing; daily prospecting discipline.
28. Francis Financial via FA-Magazine, on free-second-opinion intake as a conversion tool.

## AI-tooling positions (for the compressed AI paragraph in the strategic memo)

29. Morgan Stanley press release, "AI @ Morgan Stanley Debrief." https://www.morganstanley.com/press-releases/ai-at-morgan-stanley-debrief-launch. Advisor-facing AI deployment reporting ~30 minutes saved per client interaction and near-universal internal adoption; AI generates prompts for the advisor, not messages to the client.
30. Goldman Sachs Insights, Artificial Intelligence page. https://www.goldmansachs.com/insights/artificial-intelligence. Public position that AI empowers advisors with contextual cues and that client-facing generative AI is held back until accuracy and compliance thresholds are met.
31. Rockefeller Capital Management leadership public framing on hybrid AI-plus-advisor. https://www.rockco.com/bios/gregory-j-fleming/. Complementary position that the advisor is the relationship layer; AI supports but does not originate.

## Industry landscape and wealth-transfer grounding

32. Altrata, *The Wealthy in San Francisco 2023*. https://altrata.com/reports/wealthy-in-san-francisco-2023. SF metro UHNW population of ~4,380; 84 billionaires; seventh-highest global UHNW density per capita.
33. The Spokesman-Review, summarizing Joint Venture Silicon Valley and Kauffman Foundation data (March 2025). https://www.spokesman.com/stories/2025/mar/11/two-thirds-of-silicon-valley-tech-workers-are-fore/. ~66% foreign-born tech workforce; ~45% of Bay Area tech startups have at least one foreign-born founder.
34. Cerulli Associates, *U.S. High-Net-Worth and Ultra-High-Net-Worth Markets 2022* and related wealth-transfer projections. Approximately $84 trillion expected to transfer in the United States through 2045.
35. Cerulli Associates, "Financial Advisors Increasingly Leverage COIs to Capture New Client Growth" (U.S. Advisor Metrics 2025, released Feb 2026). https://www.cerulli.com/press-releases/financial-advisors-increasingly-leverage-cois-to-capture-new-client-growth. Referrals from clients + COIs = 54.2% + 13.9% of new client acquisition; cold outreach is not the dominant pathway to UHNW conversion.
36. Schwab Advisor Services, *2025 RIA Benchmarking Study*. https://advisorservices.schwab.com/insights-hub/perspectives/ria-benchmarking-study-2025. Top-performing advisors: 81% have documented ideal client profile and value proposition; warm introductions dominate client acquisition.
37. Knight Frank, *The Wealth Report 2025*. https://www.knightfrank.com/wealthreport. Global UHNW distribution and wealth-transfer dynamics.
38. Altrata, *World Ultra Wealth Report 2024/2025*. https://altrata.com/reports/world-ultra-wealth-report-2025. Global UHNW population breakdown by region and wealth source.
