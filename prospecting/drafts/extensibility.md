## Extensibility: The schema beyond Bay Area tech

The four prospects above live in Bay Area tech. The schema itself generalizes. Categories stay stable across verticals; specific fields swap to match the archetype.

For a **Latin American multi-generational family office**, the *Liquidity event history* field becomes *Family-business operating entity and exit posture* (most wealth stays in operating assets, not in discrete liquidity events), and *Ties to home country* expands to *Jurisdictional footprint across generations*, tracking the pattern common to families with branches across Mexico City, Miami, Madrid, and Bogotá.

For a **legacy industrial family**, *Professional history and board work* becomes *Family council role and operating-company seat*, and the *Philanthropic interests* field is reframed around the century-old foundation the family likely already operates.

For a **specialty medical practice family**, *Asset concentration* shifts from founder stock to practice equity and real estate; *Conversational signals* pick up practice-succession language (partnership buy-in timing, whether a child will take over the practice) that drives outreach toward continuity, not diversification.

The rule is the same across verticals: if a field cannot drive an outreach decision, it does not belong in the schema.
