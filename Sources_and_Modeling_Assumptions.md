## Return Scenario Assumptions

The 20-year projection model uses three scenarios. These are not forecasts. They represent a range of historical and forward-looking return estimates drawn from long-run capital market research.

| Scenario | Net Annual Return | Basis |
|---|---|---|
| Bear | 3.0% | Approximates a low-growth, elevated-volatility environment; consistent with historical fixed income and defensive equity blended returns over extended bear cycles |
| Base | 7.8% | Aligned with long-run blended portfolio return assumptions from JPMorgan Asset Management Long-Term Capital Market Assumptions and Vanguard Capital Markets Model projections for a diversified 55/23/12/8/2 multi-asset allocation |
| Bull | 11.5% | Reflects a sustained risk-on, high-growth environment; consistent with upper-decile historical equity return periods (e.g., 1990s, 2010s) applied to a portfolio with meaningful equity and private equity exposure |

All scenarios net out a 2.0% annual withdrawal rate applied to the opening portfolio balance.

**Reference sources for return assumptions:**
- JPMorgan Asset Management. *Long-Term Capital Market Assumptions*. Published annually.
- Vanguard Research. *Vanguard Capital Markets Model (VCMM)*. Published periodically.
- Damodaran, A. *Historical Returns on Stocks, Bonds, and Bills: United States*. NYU Stern School of Business. Updated annually.

## Portfolio Risk Metrics: Methodology Notes

All risk metrics in the case study are modeled estimates, not live portfolio calculations. They are constructed to illustrate the directional impact of diversification.

| Metric | Methodology |
|---|---|
| Portfolio Beta | Weighted average beta of holdings relative to the S&P 500; tech concentration beta derived from historical sector beta data (MSCI, Bloomberg) |
| S&P 500 Correlation | Estimated based on holding-level correlation to SPX; high-concentration tech portfolios historically exhibit 0.90-0.95 correlation |
| Value at Risk (95%) | Parametric VaR calculated over a one-year horizon using portfolio standard deviation; does not capture tail risk beyond the 95th percentile |
| Maximum Drawdown | Based on historical peak-to-trough drawdowns for comparable portfolios; 45% for concentrated large-cap tech references 2000-2002 and 2022 tech sector drawdowns; 22% for diversified multi-asset portfolios references post-2008 balanced portfolio behavior |
| Sharpe Ratio | Calculated using modeled return assumptions and estimated portfolio volatility; assumes a risk-free rate of 5.0% (approximate 90-day T-bill yield at time of modeling) |
| Concentration Risk Score | Proprietary illustrative index; scaled 0-100 based on Herfindahl-Hirschman Index (HHI) logic applied to position weights |
